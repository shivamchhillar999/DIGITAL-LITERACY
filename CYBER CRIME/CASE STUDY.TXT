Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of cybercrime where scammers trick individuals into authorizing payments or revealing sensitive banking details. These frauds are increasingly common due to the widespread use of mobile payment apps.

A typical scam begins when a fraudster contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, delivery agent, or customer support representative. For example, in a realistic scenario, a college student named Rahul receives a call claiming he has won a cashback reward. The caller asks him to click on a link or approve a “collect request” on his UPI app to receive the money. Believing it to be genuine, Rahul enters his UPI PIN to approve the request. Instead of receiving money, the amount gets debited from his account. In some cases, scammers also send fake QR codes or phishing links to gain access to bank details.

These frauds usually target students, elderly people, and individuals who are less aware of digital payment security practices. People who frequently use online transactions without verifying sources are especially vulnerable.

The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, emotional stress, and compromised personal data. Victims may find it difficult to recover their money, especially if the fraud is not reported immediately.

This case highlights the importance of awareness and caution while using UPI services. Users should never share their UPI PIN, approve unknown payment requests, or trust unsolicited calls and messages.
